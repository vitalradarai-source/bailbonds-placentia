Placentia Domestic Violence Bail Bonds: What Happens After an Arrest

A domestic violence arrest in Placentia moves fast. Within hours, a loved one can go from home to a holding cell at the Orange County Jail. Knowing what happens at each step — who responds, where the defendant is taken, and how bail works — helps families act quickly and get their family member home sooner.

What Happens at a Domestic Violence Arrest in Placentia

Placentia Police Department (PPD) handles all domestic violence calls within city limits. California's mandatory arrest law (Penal Code § 836(c)) requires officers to make an arrest when there is probable cause to believe domestic violence occurred. Officers do not need to witness the incident firsthand.

When PPD responds to a DV call, officers will:

  • Separate the parties and conduct individual interviews
  • Document visible injuries and photograph the scene
  • Determine the primary aggressor based on witness statements and physical evidence
  • Place the defendant in handcuffs and transport them to the Placentia Police Department facility for initial processing
  • Issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) on the spot, which takes effect immediately

Even if the alleged victim does not want to press charges, the arrest proceeds. The decision to charge rests with the Orange County District Attorney, not the individual involved.

Booking at Theo Lacy Facility in Orange

After initial processing at Placentia PD, defendants charged with domestic violence are transferred to the Theo Lacy Facility (320 N. Flower St., Orange, CA 92868), the primary booking facility for North Orange County. Theo Lacy is operated by the Orange County Sheriff's Department and handles the majority of arrests from Placentia, Anaheim, Fullerton, and surrounding cities.

The full booking process at Theo Lacy typically takes 2 to 6 hours from the time of arrival, though peak periods can extend that timeline. During booking, staff will:

  • Record personal information and photograph the defendant
  • Take fingerprints and run a criminal background check
  • Record the charges and assign a bail amount per the Orange County bail schedule
  • Place the defendant in a holding area until bail is posted or arraignment occurs

How to Post Bail for a Domestic Violence Arrest in Placentia

Most families cannot pay the full bail amount in cash. That amount is non-refundable regardless of case outcome.

Protective Orders and Bail — What You Need to Know

One of the most common misunderstandings in domestic violence cases is the relationship between protective orders and bail. An Emergency Protective Order (EPO) is issued by the arresting officer at the time of arrest. It restricts contact with the protected person but does not prevent the defendant from posting bail and being released.

The EPO is a separate civil matter from the criminal bail process. A defendant can be released on bail the same day as arrest while an EPO remains in effect. Violating the EPO after release, however, is a separate criminal offense.

At the first court appearance — typically arraignment at the North Justice Center (1275 N. Berkeley Ave., Fullerton) — the judge may convert the EPO to a Criminal Protective Order (CPO) under PC § 136.2, which can last for the duration of the case. The bail bond remains valid throughout this process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take at Theo Lacy after a Placentia DV arrest?

Booking at Theo Lacy Facility in Orange typically takes 2 to 6 hours. The timeline depends on how busy the facility is when the defendant arrives. During busy periods — weekend nights and holidays — waits can be longer. We recommend calling Angels Bail Bonds immediately after arrest so we can begin the process before booking is complete.

Can bail be posted immediately after a domestic violence arrest in Placentia?

Yes. Bail can be posted as soon as the booking process is complete and a bail amount has been assigned. There is no mandatory waiting period after a domestic violence arrest in California. The defendant does not need to wait for a court hearing to be released on bail.

How much does a bail bondsman charge for a $50,000 DV bail?

California law sets the bail bond premium at 10% of the total bail amount. For a $50,000 PC § 273.5 bail, the premium is $5,000. This fee goes to the bail bond company and is not returned after the case concludes. Angels Bail Bonds offers payment plans for qualified clients.

Can the victim drop charges to get bail reduced or released faster?

No. In California, domestic violence charges are prosecuted by the District Attorney — not the alleged victim. The victim can choose not to cooperate with the prosecution, but cannot unilaterally drop charges or influence the bail amount. Bail is determined by the Orange County bail schedule and can only be formally adjusted by a judge at arraignment.
